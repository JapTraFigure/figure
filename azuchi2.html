Doyusha's 1/550 scale Azuchi Castle [2/3]

The first step is to paint the stone walls.

This is the base of what will become the stone wall.

Paint the edges of the base and the stone wall areas with semi-gloss black. Paint the base with neutral gray without masking. At this time, paint randomly to make the previously painted black transparent.

This is where the daunting task begins. Neutral gray, white, black, brown, and other paints are put out on a palette and mixed appropriately to create colors, which are then chomped on and applied to the stones of the stone wall.

There are many different types of stones, so we are painting as we go. It is very time-consuming, but let's do it little by little. My wife said, "Let me do that work too!" I asked her to help me paint a little. She is very dexterous. She is good at it.

Smear the stone wall. Apply enamel flat black, thinned to a shabby consistency, over the entire surface with a brush.

Once dry, wipe off the flat black with a cotton swab dipped in enamel solvent.

The flat black color will then remain only in the depressed areas of the stone wall, giving the stone wall a three-dimensional appearance.

Like this.

If you place the unpainted main circle, the difference completely stands out.

Next, we will apply the masking tape for painting the soil area. First, apply masking tape to cover the stone wall.

Cut the tape with a design knife and remove it with tweezers. Part of the stone wall masking is completed. This process is repeated. Plastic modeling is a repetition of steady work.
