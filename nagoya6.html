Doyusha's 1/350 scale Nagoya Castle [6/6]

The kit includes parts to make a pine tree, but the trunk is simple and the texture is nowhere near as good as it should be for how hard it looks to assemble. This time, we tried to find an easier way.

This time, let's use "KUROMATSU" from the Geo-Collections and Scenic Collections released by Tomytec!

This "KUROMATSU" is 1/150 scale, so it does not fit the 1/350 scale Nagoya Castle as it is. However, if the branches are cut off, the tree can be used as a tree that fits the scale.

This wooden material is a material that can be bent to a certain degree to create the shape of a tree.

Using wood glue, we will affix the sponge for leaves included in the "KUROMATSU" kit.

Some trees have been created, although they no longer look like pine trees.

The holes for mounting the wood were filled in, so holes were drilled from the back and penetrated through to the front.

The hole was a little big, so I used epoxy bond purchased at a 100 yen store to hold it in place stiffly. It hardens in about 5 minutes, so when it is just about to harden, I insert the wood and stand it up.

If you are concerned about the hole marks, sprinkle colored powder around the hole again and fix it with water-soluble wood glue.

The main and small keepers no longer fit into the stone wall, so the protrusion of the mounting guide will be scraped off to make it flat.

The surface of the castle tower is adjusted with paper to match the stone wall so that there are no gaps.

Clear bond was used for gluing the keepers, which is easy to fix if they stick out.

The keep and other parts are attached, and finally each roof is drybrushed with a bright color to give it a three-dimensional appearance.
