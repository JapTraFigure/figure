Doyusha's 1/350 scale Osaka Castle [5/8]

It is not easy just to temporarily assemble it, so we are making various modifications, but we have managed to get the right atmosphere.

The five layers are quite voluminous when stacked like this.

The roof is wavy, and where it cannot be corrected in any way, it is cut off and reshaped with plastic plates.

Where the torsion is great, we added beams like this.

This kit is for the reconstruction keep on hand.

The reconstruction keep refers to the current Osaka Castle. This reconstruction keep is a very interesting castle that is controversial.

The first four stories are white plaster following the Tokugawa Osaka Castle, the top layer is black plaster following the Toyotomi Osaka Castle, the shape is Toyotomi Osaka Castle, and the stone walls are hammered hagi from the Tokugawa period.

This can be clearly seen in the parts shown here.

It is called Chidori-hafu.The detail carved in this red circle is the Toyotomi family crest, Goshichi-kiri (57 paulownia).

In other words, the combination of Tokugawa white plaster and Toyotomi's family crest is an impossible combination.

Once you understand the background of the castle, you will realize the depth and interest of the castle.
