Himeji Castle remodeled to look like a ruin [2/5]

Creative work in progress, Castle Himeji in the Sky. Painting of stone walls.

I placed the keep and the scratched five turrets to check the atmosphere. The high stone wall under the turret in the band in the foreground is the tallest in the castle, almost 24 meters. I wanted to create this. Continuing with the primer, although it is dirty. This time, the color is darker because it is for the ruins. The dark brown areas will all be green later.

The process is a little different for the abandoned building. This time, the white walls of the building will be attached to the foundation.

This is because the white walls are covered with ivy and moss.

Buildings (man-made) eroding into the foundation (natural). The scratch building has to go on at the same time, so the work goes here and there. It's a right-and-left situation. At any rate, we have reached the wall of the turrets in the Bizen Maru.

Collapsed turrets in the Kamiyamari-maru. The base of the Later it will be covered with greenery.

The Honmaru residence, whose large roof can be seen in the old photo, is intended to grow trees from the fallen roof. In the historical fact, it was burnt down before it got that rough. I placed the roof temporarily.

It still has a strong plastic model feel.
