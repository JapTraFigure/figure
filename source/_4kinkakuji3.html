Fujimi's 1/100 scale Kinkakuji Temple [3/3]

This time, the kit of the Golden Pavilion is completed.

Paint the pond stones. We have a glossy white, black, German gray, wood brown, red brown, and ship's bottom color of CREOS lacquer-based paint, but you can paint the stones a little bluish or reddish without thinking too much about the details. You can paint this part in any color you like as you see fit.

The base of the building, including the stones, and the first layer, which is not gilded, should be washed with low-amber oil paint diluted with petrol. If you do not have petrol or oil paint, you can use Tamiya Color enamel solvent and enamel paint.

Drybrush the stone and wood areas with a slightly lighter color to emphasize the three-dimensional effect.

The joints of the cobblestones were smudged using a 0.3mm mechanical pencil.

The "body" of the "aisle" does not fit perfectly with the main body. A large gap was created at the bottom of the passageway, so I cut off the part and remade it with plastic plates.

The ends of the beams were painted white based on the photo.

The roof also does not quite match the walls. I should have adjusted it by assembling it temporarily with the wall a little more firmly at the previous stage. Please be careful if you are going to make a new roof. It was a bit messy, but I glued it forcibly with instant adhesives.

I tried smudging in this area but it felt dirty, so I wiped it off. This time, I decided to leave the gold part as it was without any smudging.

The roof was lightly dry brushed with Tamiya's Weathering Master Gray to emphasize the corners where the four surfaces meet, but the surfaces were lightly rubbed to avoid overdoing it.

Finally, the pond area is polished with compound to remove any hand marks.

The instructions instructed us to cut out the letters from the instruction manual and paste them on the nameplate, but we printed the letters on gold inkjet paper and attached them because black-and-white printed materials are tasteless.
