Doyusha's 1/350 scale Kumamoto Castle [6/7]

Continuation and finishing of the scene (diorama) production

After painting most of the stone walls, we will build up the ground.

For the exposed soil, I used Tamiya Scenic Texture Paint: Dirt Dark Earth.

This has a nice texture and color tone!

The texture and color of the soil looked great, and the crumbly texture of the soil didn't feel too strange, so I'll just go ahead with the project.

I used Tamiya Scenic Texture Paint: Road Surface Light Gray for the graveled areas, but it looks awfully white.... (the area indicated by the arrow in the image)

I used it without stirring.

It looks like snow is piled up on the ground, so I used Tamiya Scene Texture Paint: Dark Gray for the road surface to correct it.

Also, I noticed that there is a passage to the left when you go up the stairs, but it dead-ends at the end of the passage....

It feels very strange to me....

Maybe my interpretation is wrong, but it would be very difficult to correct it at this stage, so I decided not to look here.

For the garden area, I used Tamiya Scenic Texture Paint: Sand Light Sand.

This one has finer particles than the earth dark earth, which was perfect for the situation I was aiming for.

However, I felt the color was a little too bright, so I washed it down with Mr. Weathering Color: Grand Brown, which I diluted to a crisp to calm it down.

After finishing the ground, I used the paints in AK interactive: DECAY ABANDONED WEATHERING SET to express weeds and mosses, mainly in areas like between the soil and the stone walls.

These paints are also made by a foreign manufacturer and are very useful, but they are a bit expensive and hard to find....

However, it was fun to try different kinds of paints this time.

Turf for model trains was used to recreate the lawn.

The powder included in the kit would have been fine, but the amount was too small and I was not sure if it would be enough, so I used the turf I had prepared in advance.

However, this one has slightly larger fibers and I felt it might spoil the scale.

Compared to the above turf, the one in the kit has smaller fibers, which is probably quite good....

Also, it is important to note that the kit provides two colors of powder.

Incidentally, the instructions for Kumamoto Castle instruct you to sprinkle the powder on the ground before the paint dries to fix it in place.

However, that leaves some uncertainty about fixing it in place, so here I used matte medium dissolved in water to fix it in place.

I found it easier to control the turf by applying the water-dissolved matte medium to the area where I wanted to plant the turf in advance and sprinkling the turf on top of the matte medium.

I felt that if I only planted trees and turf, the height would be extreme and unbalanced, so I decided to plant low trees as well.

First of all, I will place a richen for model trains....

Hmmm, what is it, but to me it looks like a monster summoned from another world suddenly appeared....

It's kind of creepy, so I rejected this one....

The next one I used was called foliage, also sold for model trains, which is a sponge material attached to a net-like fiber.

Yes, this one is definitely better...

Finally, I planted a tree, but I thought it would be unnatural for it to grow suddenly out of the ground, so I sprinkled Forridge's sponge around the base of the tree.
