This time, we are painting the base stone wall section.

Airbrush the neutral gray.

Masking the stone wall area and painting the ground area. Masking of the stone wall area is done with a brush, cutting out details.
Painting the ground

  • Red brown: Spray mainly on the boundary between the stone wall and the ground.
  • Desert yellow: Lightly spray on the entire ground area.
  • Tan: Lightly paint on the ground part.
  • Buffing: To give the appearance of soil, we will add more layers of buffing.
Now we are expressing the ground with these 4 coats, but how will it turn out?

The lawn may be sprayed green later to recreate the lawn.

I'll remove the masking. In case you're wondering, I painted the grassy areas dark green.

If you paint it with masking, it looks more like a block wall than a stone wall.

It is still difficult to express the ruggedness of the wall.

This is a tough angle.

I will apply buffed paint one stone wall at a time with a brush for trial and error. I will also try painting a diluted neutral gray color and a famous grayish-white color. I will try different colors, because I know that no two stones will have the same look.

Is it better than a block wall?

Further color will be added and final inking will be applied. To put it bluntly. It's 10 times harder than car modeling! But it is fun to be able to pursue various expressions. It's such a lot of work just to make a stone wall. What about the lawn? How to express the white walls? How to express wooden parts? How to express roof tiles? There are a lot of issues that car models don't have, and it is really challenging to build.
