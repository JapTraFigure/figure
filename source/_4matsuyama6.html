Doyusha's 1/450 scale Maysuyama Castle [6/6]

Matsuyama Castle has finally been completed. Here is the gallery.

Low angle.

It was very difficult to paint the stone walls one by one.

I was also particular about painting the white walls and boarded areas.

The symbol of Matsuyama City, Ehime Prefecture

This is a panoramic view.

The size is about this big. I did not use turf powder, which is a common ingredient in plastic model castles, this time. I did not use it this time because I did not think the amount of turf powder was enough and the single-color powder would make the model look like a toy. If you can use a variety of powders, it is possible.

Black background.

The structure of the keep.

Panoramic view.

How was it? Anyway, the parts are small and don't fit well, so it was a lot of hard work. There are many tricks to building an architectural model that are not found in car models. I think that if you dig out the lines in the stone wall, you can make it look more three-dimensional. I would also like to plant small trees.
The finished product stands out in the room. It has too much presence. I recommend the "Plastic model of a castle" full of historical romance.
