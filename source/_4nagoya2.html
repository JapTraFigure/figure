Doyusha's 1/350 scale Nagoya Castle [2/6]

Now, as for the Nagoya Castle, we lightly spray the surfacer with a can sprayer to check for gaps and scratches on the parts.

This is the primer coat for the parts. The walls are white, but I painted them with German gray can spray paint. White molded parts let light through more than expected. Even if you think you have painted them, just a thin coat of a bright color such as white may not have enough hiding power, and light may pass through slightly, resulting in a somewhat light finish. Also, the interior is empty, so we want to keep the interior dark so that it is not noticeable. Therefore, we first paint the white walls in a dark color both inside and outside to create a stately finish.

The stone walls and other base areas were also sprayed with German gray as well.

The roof was primed in red brown on both the back and front.
