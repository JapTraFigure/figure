Roofs of castles are not green.

Roofs of castles and temples are often green, but that is the color of rusted copper. If you think of an old 10-yen coin, you can get the general idea.

Bronze rusts to form a film that is semi-permanent (for the life of the building) and durable.

This is evidenced by the fact that bronze vessels and bronze statues from the past are still in existence.

Now, let us return to the model. Toyotomi Osaka Castle was a short-lived castle, falling about 30 years after it was built, from 1583 to 1615. Therefore, it is thought that the roof of Osaka Castle had hardly reached the bronze color before it was exposed to warfare.
